# Customer Success

## Overview

This category tests understanding of post-sale outcomes, adoption, retention, and expansion in partnership with CS teams.

## Why Interviewers Ask This

Interviewers assess lifecycle thinking beyond initial close.

## Key Concepts

- Onboarding success
- Adoption metrics
- Renewal readiness
- Expansion identification
- Churn prevention

## Common Interview Questions

1. How does BD work with customer success after handoff?
2. How do you ensure promises made in sales are deliverable?
3. How do you identify expansion opportunities ethically?
4. How do you prevent churn from poor expectation-setting?
5. What signals indicate renewal risk?
6. How do you run joint account reviews with CS?
7. How do you balance new logo focus with existing accounts?
8. How do you use customer outcomes in future sales?
9. How do you manage escalations in strategic accounts?
10. Share a customer lifecycle win involving BD and CS.

## Example Answers

- Effective BD includes accountable handoff and shared success metrics with customer success teams.
- I prevent churn by setting realistic expectations and documenting success criteria before contract signature.
- Expansion should follow demonstrated value, not aggressive upsell pressure.

## Real Business Scenarios

- A high-risk account renewed after BD and CS aligned on adoption milestones and executive alignment plan.
- Customer outcome stories became strong social proof for net-new acquisition.

## Best Practices

- Use a clear structure: context, action, outcome, learning.
- Quantify impact with metrics such as conversion rate, revenue, cycle time, or retention.
- Show buyer empathy and business understanding, not just sales tactics.
- Tie your answer to role expectations and company stage.
- Keep responses concise, then expand if asked.

## Mistakes to Avoid

- Giving generic answers without numbers.
- Over-focusing on activity volume instead of business outcomes.
- Criticizing past teams or customers.
- Ignoring cross-functional alignment with marketing, product, and customer success.
- Not asking clarifying questions in scenario prompts.
