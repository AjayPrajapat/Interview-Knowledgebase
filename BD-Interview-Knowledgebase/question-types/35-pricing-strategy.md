# Pricing Strategy

## Overview

This category evaluates pricing fluency in value communication, discount governance, and commercial package design.

## Why Interviewers Ask This

Interviewers test pricing judgment and revenue-quality awareness.

## Key Concepts

- Value-based pricing
- Packaging strategy
- Discount controls
- Price sensitivity by segment
- Commercial governance

## Common Interview Questions

1. How do you justify pricing to cost-focused buyers?
2. When is discounting appropriate?
3. How do you structure pricing packages?
4. How do you handle competitor undercutting?
5. How do you manage custom pricing requests?
6. How do you test pricing changes?
7. How do you protect margin while closing deals?
8. How do pricing tiers influence conversion?
9. How do you collaborate with finance on pricing policy?
10. Share a pricing decision you supported.

## Example Answers

- I anchor pricing in business outcomes and total cost impact, not feature count.
- Discounting should be tied to reciprocal value such as term commitments or expansion scope.
- Pricing strategy must balance win rate, margin, and long-term customer value.

## Real Business Scenarios

- A tiered packaging redesign improved conversion in SMB while preserving enterprise pricing power.
- Discount governance reduced end-of-quarter margin leakage.

## Best Practices

- Use a clear structure: context, action, outcome, learning.
- Quantify impact with metrics such as conversion rate, revenue, cycle time, or retention.
- Show buyer empathy and business understanding, not just sales tactics.
- Tie your answer to role expectations and company stage.
- Keep responses concise, then expand if asked.

## Mistakes to Avoid

- Giving generic answers without numbers.
- Over-focusing on activity volume instead of business outcomes.
- Criticizing past teams or customers.
- Ignoring cross-functional alignment with marketing, product, and customer success.
- Not asking clarifying questions in scenario prompts.
