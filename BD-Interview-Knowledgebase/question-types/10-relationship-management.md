# Relationship Management

## Overview

This category evaluates how candidates build trust, retain stakeholder alignment, and create long-term account value.

## Why Interviewers Ask This

Interviewers test account stewardship and ability to sustain growth beyond initial sale.

## Key Concepts

- Stakeholder mapping
- Trust-building cadence
- Executive business reviews
- Renewal and expansion readiness
- Conflict resolution

## Common Interview Questions

1. How do you build long-term client relationships?
2. How do you handle disengaged stakeholders?
3. How do you maintain relationships post-sale?
4. How do you prepare for renewal conversations?
5. How do you identify expansion opportunities?
6. How do you recover trust after delivery issues?
7. How do you run executive check-ins?
8. How do you prevent account churn?
9. How do you document relationship intelligence?
10. Share an account you grew over time.

## Example Answers

- I manage relationships through structured stakeholder maps, regular value reviews, and proactive risk communication.
- Trust is built by consistent follow-through and transparency when issues occur.
- Expansion succeeds when you align new use cases to measurable outcomes already proven in the account.

## Real Business Scenarios

- A strategic account at risk of churn renewed after quarterly outcome reviews and an issue-resolution task force.
- A mid-market client expanded to two departments after role-based onboarding.

## Best Practices

- Use a clear structure: context, action, outcome, learning.
- Quantify impact with metrics such as conversion rate, revenue, cycle time, or retention.
- Show buyer empathy and business understanding, not just sales tactics.
- Tie your answer to role expectations and company stage.
- Keep responses concise, then expand if asked.

## Mistakes to Avoid

- Giving generic answers without numbers.
- Over-focusing on activity volume instead of business outcomes.
- Criticizing past teams or customers.
- Ignoring cross-functional alignment with marketing, product, and customer success.
- Not asking clarifying questions in scenario prompts.
