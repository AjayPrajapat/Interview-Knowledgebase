# Leadership

## Overview

This category evaluates leadership potential in coaching, decision-making, and driving outcomes through teams.

## Why Interviewers Ask This

Interviewers assess ability to scale impact beyond individual contribution.

## Key Concepts

- Coaching frameworks
- Decision quality
- Team alignment
- Performance management
- Change leadership

## Common Interview Questions

1. How do you lead without formal authority?
2. How do you coach underperforming reps?
3. How do you set team priorities?
4. How do you handle disagreements in strategy?
5. How do you run effective team reviews?
6. How do you hire for BD teams?
7. How do you build accountability culture?
8. How do you motivate teams during tough quarters?
9. How do you manage high performers vs low performers?
10. Share a leadership initiative you drove.

## Example Answers

- Leadership in BD means creating systems that improve team performance, not only closing personal deals.
- I align team goals with clear metrics, coaching rhythm, and transparent decision logic.
- I address performance issues early with support plans and objective checkpoints.

## Real Business Scenarios

- A BD lead improved team conversion by implementing call reviews and persona-specific coaching tracks.
- Leadership intervention in pipeline discipline improved forecast reliability across the team.

## Best Practices

- Use a clear structure: context, action, outcome, learning.
- Quantify impact with metrics such as conversion rate, revenue, cycle time, or retention.
- Show buyer empathy and business understanding, not just sales tactics.
- Tie your answer to role expectations and company stage.
- Keep responses concise, then expand if asked.

## Mistakes to Avoid

- Giving generic answers without numbers.
- Over-focusing on activity volume instead of business outcomes.
- Criticizing past teams or customers.
- Ignoring cross-functional alignment with marketing, product, and customer success.
- Not asking clarifying questions in scenario prompts.
