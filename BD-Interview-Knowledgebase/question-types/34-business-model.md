# Business Model

## Overview

This category tests understanding of how the company creates, delivers, and captures value, and how BD supports that model.

## Why Interviewers Ask This

Interviewers assess commercial awareness and strategic fit.

## Key Concepts

- Revenue streams
- Unit economics
- Customer segments
- Channel economics
- Expansion logic

## Common Interview Questions

1. How does our business model influence BD strategy?
2. Which customer segments create highest lifetime value?
3. How do unit economics affect deal decisions?
4. How should BD adapt for subscription vs one-time sales?
5. How do channels impact margin and control?
6. How do you evaluate business-model risk?
7. How do you support recurring revenue growth?
8. How do product bundles impact model economics?
9. How do you explain business model to clients?
10. Share an example where model understanding improved sales outcomes.

## Example Answers

- BD performance improves when sellers understand the economics behind pricing, retention, and support costs.
- I prioritize opportunities that align with long-term value, not only short-term bookings.
- Business model awareness helps avoid low-margin deals that hurt company economics.

## Real Business Scenarios

- A services-heavy account strategy was redesigned toward productized offerings to improve margin and delivery consistency.
- A subscription business improved expansion plans by focusing on adoption milestones tied to renewals.

## Best Practices

- Use a clear structure: context, action, outcome, learning.
- Quantify impact with metrics such as conversion rate, revenue, cycle time, or retention.
- Show buyer empathy and business understanding, not just sales tactics.
- Tie your answer to role expectations and company stage.
- Keep responses concise, then expand if asked.

## Mistakes to Avoid

- Giving generic answers without numbers.
- Over-focusing on activity volume instead of business outcomes.
- Criticizing past teams or customers.
- Ignoring cross-functional alignment with marketing, product, and customer success.
- Not asking clarifying questions in scenario prompts.
