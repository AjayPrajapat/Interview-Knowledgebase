# Revenue Growth

## Overview

This category evaluates how candidates plan and execute growth across new logos, expansion, pricing, and retention.

## Why Interviewers Ask This

Interviewers test strategic growth thinking and commercial execution.

## Key Concepts

- Growth levers
- Expansion revenue
- Retention impact
- Segment strategy
- Revenue quality

## Common Interview Questions

1. What are your top levers for revenue growth?
2. How do you balance acquisition and expansion?
3. How do you prioritize high-growth segments?
4. How do you improve revenue quality, not just volume?
5. How do you reduce revenue concentration risk?
6. How do you build growth plans for flat markets?
7. How do you influence retention-driven growth?
8. How do you use pricing in growth strategy?
9. How do you forecast growth by channel?
10. Share a growth initiative you led.

## Example Answers

- I use a portfolio approach: strengthen core segments while testing adjacent opportunities with clear success thresholds.
- Sustainable growth comes from stronger conversion, higher retention, and expansion, not only top-of-funnel volume.
- I monitor revenue concentration and diversify accounts and verticals to reduce risk.

## Real Business Scenarios

- A team increased annual recurring revenue by combining upsell playbooks with customer success-led adoption tracking.
- A new vertical pilot became a major growth channel after proving repeatable win patterns.

## Best Practices

- Use a clear structure: context, action, outcome, learning.
- Quantify impact with metrics such as conversion rate, revenue, cycle time, or retention.
- Show buyer empathy and business understanding, not just sales tactics.
- Tie your answer to role expectations and company stage.
- Keep responses concise, then expand if asked.

## Mistakes to Avoid

- Giving generic answers without numbers.
- Over-focusing on activity volume instead of business outcomes.
- Criticizing past teams or customers.
- Ignoring cross-functional alignment with marketing, product, and customer success.
- Not asking clarifying questions in scenario prompts.
