# Problem Solving

## Overview

This category tests analytical problem-solving on revenue blockers, process inefficiencies, and execution gaps.

## Why Interviewers Ask This

Interviewers evaluate diagnosis quality and practical solution design.

## Key Concepts

- Root-cause methods
- Prioritization frameworks
- Experiment design
- Stakeholder alignment
- Execution follow-through

## Common Interview Questions

1. Describe your approach to solving a persistent conversion issue.
2. How do you diagnose pipeline stagnation?
3. How do you identify whether problem is message, market, or process?
4. How do you prioritize fixes with limited resources?
5. How do you test solutions quickly?
6. How do you know a fix worked?
7. How do you involve cross-functional teams in problem solving?
8. How do you handle incomplete data?
9. Share a difficult problem you solved in BD.
10. What is your escalation approach for unresolved blockers?

## Example Answers

- I separate symptoms from causes by mapping stage-level data and qualitative call insights.
- I propose low-cost tests first, then scale what proves impact.
- I define success metrics upfront so solution effectiveness is measurable.

## Real Business Scenarios

- A team solved low SQL quality by redesigning lead scoring and discovery scripts.
- Proposal delays were fixed by introducing legal pre-review templates and owner SLAs.

## Best Practices

- Use a clear structure: context, action, outcome, learning.
- Quantify impact with metrics such as conversion rate, revenue, cycle time, or retention.
- Show buyer empathy and business understanding, not just sales tactics.
- Tie your answer to role expectations and company stage.
- Keep responses concise, then expand if asked.

## Mistakes to Avoid

- Giving generic answers without numbers.
- Over-focusing on activity volume instead of business outcomes.
- Criticizing past teams or customers.
- Ignoring cross-functional alignment with marketing, product, and customer success.
- Not asking clarifying questions in scenario prompts.
