# Strategy

## Overview

This category evaluates strategic planning ability for revenue growth, channel selection, and execution prioritization.

## Why Interviewers Ask This

Interviewers assess whether candidates can set direction, choose focus areas, and align teams around measurable goals.

## Key Concepts

- Growth hypotheses
- Strategic prioritization
- Segment/channel selection
- Resource allocation
- KPI framework

## Common Interview Questions

1. How do you build a BD strategy for a new fiscal year?
2. Which growth levers do you prioritize first?
3. How do you allocate resources across segments?
4. How do you define strategic KPIs?
5. How do you test strategy assumptions?
6. How do you respond to underperforming strategy?
7. How do you align stakeholders behind strategy changes?
8. How do you evaluate channel ROI?
9. What makes strategy executable?
10. Share a strategy that failed and what you changed.

## Example Answers

- My BD strategy starts with baseline funnel metrics, then identifies 2-3 highest-leverage experiments tied to revenue goals.
- I convert strategy into execution by defining owners, timelines, and stage-level KPIs.
- I run monthly strategy reviews and rebalance resources based on evidence, not opinions.

## Real Business Scenarios

- A regional strategy was revised from broad outreach to vertical specialization and produced faster pipeline growth.
- A company expanded partner channel only after proving direct motion economics first.

## Best Practices

- Use a clear structure: context, action, outcome, learning.
- Quantify impact with metrics such as conversion rate, revenue, cycle time, or retention.
- Show buyer empathy and business understanding, not just sales tactics.
- Tie your answer to role expectations and company stage.
- Keep responses concise, then expand if asked.

## Mistakes to Avoid

- Giving generic answers without numbers.
- Over-focusing on activity volume instead of business outcomes.
- Criticizing past teams or customers.
- Ignoring cross-functional alignment with marketing, product, and customer success.
- Not asking clarifying questions in scenario prompts.
