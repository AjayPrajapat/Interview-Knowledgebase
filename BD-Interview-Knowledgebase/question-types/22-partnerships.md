# Partnerships

## Overview

This category tests ability to source, evaluate, and manage strategic partnerships that accelerate revenue and market access.

## Why Interviewers Ask This

Interviewers evaluate partnership economics, governance, and execution.

## Key Concepts

- Partner types and models
- Co-sell motions
- Incentive alignment
- Partner onboarding
- Joint success metrics

## Common Interview Questions

1. How do you identify good strategic partners?
2. What criteria determine partnership viability?
3. How do you structure partner incentives?
4. How do you launch a co-sell motion?
5. How do you manage partner conflicts?
6. How do you evaluate partner ROI?
7. How do you avoid low-commitment partnerships?
8. How do you run partner enablement?
9. How do you set governance for partnerships?
10. Share a partnership that drove measurable growth.

## Example Answers

- I evaluate partners on customer overlap, execution capability, and economic alignment before formalizing terms.
- Successful partnerships require operational rhythm: enablement, pipeline reviews, and shared accountability.
- I track sourced pipeline, influenced revenue, and partner activation metrics.

## Real Business Scenarios

- A technology alliance increased win rate in regulated accounts by bundling compliance services.
- A channel partnership failed due to unclear ownership and weak enablement, then was redesigned with clear KPIs.

## Best Practices

- Use a clear structure: context, action, outcome, learning.
- Quantify impact with metrics such as conversion rate, revenue, cycle time, or retention.
- Show buyer empathy and business understanding, not just sales tactics.
- Tie your answer to role expectations and company stage.
- Keep responses concise, then expand if asked.

## Mistakes to Avoid

- Giving generic answers without numbers.
- Over-focusing on activity volume instead of business outcomes.
- Criticizing past teams or customers.
- Ignoring cross-functional alignment with marketing, product, and customer success.
- Not asking clarifying questions in scenario prompts.
