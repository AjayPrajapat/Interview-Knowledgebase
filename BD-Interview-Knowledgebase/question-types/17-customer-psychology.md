# Customer Psychology

## Overview

This category evaluates how candidates understand buyer motivations, risk perception, and decision behavior.

## Why Interviewers Ask This

Interviewers test empathy, persuasion ethics, and message timing.

## Key Concepts

- Buying triggers
- Risk aversion
- Social proof influence
- Decision fatigue
- Trust mechanisms

## Common Interview Questions

1. What psychological factors influence B2B purchase decisions?
2. How do you reduce perceived risk for buyers?
3. How do you build trust early in a relationship?
4. How do you identify emotional vs rational objections?
5. How do you avoid pressure-based selling?
6. How do you use social proof effectively?
7. How do you handle indecisive buyers?
8. How do buyer personas differ by seniority?
9. How do you improve message resonance?
10. Share a deal where understanding psychology changed outcome.

## Example Answers

- Buyers need confidence in business outcome and implementation safety, not just product capability.
- I reduce risk by providing references, phased plans, and explicit success criteria.
- Understanding motivation helps tailor calls-to-action that feel relevant rather than pushy.

## Real Business Scenarios

- A procurement-led deal progressed after reframing from feature comparison to cost-of-delay and compliance assurance.
- A skeptical decision-maker converted after peer-customer reference calls.

## Best Practices

- Use a clear structure: context, action, outcome, learning.
- Quantify impact with metrics such as conversion rate, revenue, cycle time, or retention.
- Show buyer empathy and business understanding, not just sales tactics.
- Tie your answer to role expectations and company stage.
- Keep responses concise, then expand if asked.

## Mistakes to Avoid

- Giving generic answers without numbers.
- Over-focusing on activity volume instead of business outcomes.
- Criticizing past teams or customers.
- Ignoring cross-functional alignment with marketing, product, and customer success.
- Not asking clarifying questions in scenario prompts.
