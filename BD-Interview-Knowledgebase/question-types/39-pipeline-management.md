# Pipeline Management

## Overview

This category tests ability to maintain a healthy, balanced pipeline with clear stage progression and predictable outcomes.

## Why Interviewers Ask This

Interviewers evaluate operational consistency and risk control.

## Key Concepts

- Pipeline coverage
- Stage progression discipline
- Deal aging controls
- Pipeline hygiene
- Review rhythm

## Common Interview Questions

1. What does a healthy pipeline look like?
2. How do you determine required pipeline coverage?
3. How do you manage stale opportunities?
4. How do you prioritize pipeline reviews?
5. How do you balance pipeline building and closing work?
6. How do you identify hidden pipeline risk?
7. How do you improve stage velocity?
8. How do you run deal inspections effectively?
9. How do you manage pipeline quality across reps?
10. Share a pipeline process improvement you led.

## Example Answers

- Pipeline management is about quality and progression, not headline amount alone.
- I track coverage, stage conversion, and aging trends to anticipate shortfalls early.
- Strong pipeline reviews focus on evidence-backed next steps and blocker removal.

## Real Business Scenarios

- A team improved quarter predictability by removing stale deals and enforcing stage exit criteria.
- Pipeline coverage recovered through segment-focused prospecting sprints.

## Best Practices

- Use a clear structure: context, action, outcome, learning.
- Quantify impact with metrics such as conversion rate, revenue, cycle time, or retention.
- Show buyer empathy and business understanding, not just sales tactics.
- Tie your answer to role expectations and company stage.
- Keep responses concise, then expand if asked.

## Mistakes to Avoid

- Giving generic answers without numbers.
- Over-focusing on activity volume instead of business outcomes.
- Criticizing past teams or customers.
- Ignoring cross-functional alignment with marketing, product, and customer success.
- Not asking clarifying questions in scenario prompts.
