# Forecasting

## Overview

This category evaluates ability to forecast revenue accurately and communicate confidence, risk, and scenario plans.

## Why Interviewers Ask This

Interviewers test analytical discipline and executive communication.

## Key Concepts

- Forecast categories
- Probability calibration
- Stage aging risk
- Scenario planning
- Forecast governance cadence

## Common Interview Questions

1. How do you build your forecast each week?
2. How do you assign deal probabilities?
3. What causes forecast inaccuracy?
4. How do you communicate forecast risk to leadership?
5. How do you handle optimistic bias in pipeline reviews?
6. How do you forecast in a new territory with limited history?
7. How do you use stage aging in forecast logic?
8. How do you create best/base/worst-case forecasts?
9. How do you improve forecast reliability over time?
10. Share a time your forecast was wrong and what changed.

## Example Answers

- I forecast from deal evidence, not optimism: stakeholder access, next-step quality, legal status, and timing realism.
- I maintain scenario ranges and make risk assumptions explicit.
- Forecast quality improves with strict stage criteria and disciplined pipeline hygiene.

## Real Business Scenarios

- A team reduced forecast variance by standardizing close-plan templates and weekly deal review checkpoints.
- Scenario forecasting helped management adjust spend during demand slowdown.

## Best Practices

- Use a clear structure: context, action, outcome, learning.
- Quantify impact with metrics such as conversion rate, revenue, cycle time, or retention.
- Show buyer empathy and business understanding, not just sales tactics.
- Tie your answer to role expectations and company stage.
- Keep responses concise, then expand if asked.

## Mistakes to Avoid

- Giving generic answers without numbers.
- Over-focusing on activity volume instead of business outcomes.
- Criticizing past teams or customers.
- Ignoring cross-functional alignment with marketing, product, and customer success.
- Not asking clarifying questions in scenario prompts.
