# Market Research

## Overview

This category covers how candidates assess market size, segments, competitors, and demand signals to shape BD priorities.

## Why Interviewers Ask This

Interviewers test analytical thinking and ability to convert market insights into actionable account strategy.

## Key Concepts

- TAM/SAM/SOM
- Competitive intelligence
- Segment attractiveness
- Buyer trend analysis
- Opportunity prioritization

## Common Interview Questions

1. How do you research a new market?
2. Which data sources do you trust and why?
3. How do you estimate market potential?
4. How do you analyze competitors?
5. How do you prioritize segments?
6. How do you validate demand assumptions?
7. How do you detect market shifts early?
8. How does research influence outreach messaging?
9. How do you combine qualitative and quantitative inputs?
10. Share a decision improved by market research.

## Example Answers

- I use a hypothesis-driven approach: estimate opportunity, validate with customer signals, then iterate territory focus.
- Competitive research should include pricing, positioning, deal motion, and implementation strengths.
- Good market research directly informs ICP refinements and messaging priorities.

## Real Business Scenarios

- A SaaS team shifted from broad SMB targeting to a high-intent niche based on win/loss and usage patterns.
- Market trend analysis helped a services firm launch a new compliance package ahead of demand.

## Best Practices

- Use a clear structure: context, action, outcome, learning.
- Quantify impact with metrics such as conversion rate, revenue, cycle time, or retention.
- Show buyer empathy and business understanding, not just sales tactics.
- Tie your answer to role expectations and company stage.
- Keep responses concise, then expand if asked.

## Mistakes to Avoid

- Giving generic answers without numbers.
- Over-focusing on activity volume instead of business outcomes.
- Criticizing past teams or customers.
- Ignoring cross-functional alignment with marketing, product, and customer success.
- Not asking clarifying questions in scenario prompts.
