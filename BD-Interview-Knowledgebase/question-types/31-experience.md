# Experience

## Overview

This category explores prior role scope, achievements, failures, and transferable BD capabilities.

## Why Interviewers Ask This

Interviewers validate depth, relevance, and credibility of past performance.

## Key Concepts

- Scope and ownership
- Measurable achievements
- Role progression
- Lessons from failures
- Transferability

## Common Interview Questions

1. Walk me through your BD experience.
2. What was your biggest achievement in the last role?
3. What deals are you most proud of and why?
4. What did you own end-to-end?
5. How did your responsibilities evolve?
6. What was your most difficult account?
7. What mistake taught you the most?
8. Which industry experience is most relevant here?
9. How do your past metrics compare year over year?
10. What would your previous manager say about you?

## Example Answers

- My experience combines outbound pipeline creation, enterprise discovery, and cross-functional deal execution.
- I focus on outcomes with context: baseline, intervention, result, and repeatability.
- I highlight lessons from misses and how I operationalized improvements.

## Real Business Scenarios

- A candidate transitioned from SMB to enterprise by showing transferable process rigor and stakeholder management depth.
- Experience in regulated sectors helped reduce sales cycle risk in compliance-heavy deals.

## Best Practices

- Use a clear structure: context, action, outcome, learning.
- Quantify impact with metrics such as conversion rate, revenue, cycle time, or retention.
- Show buyer empathy and business understanding, not just sales tactics.
- Tie your answer to role expectations and company stage.
- Keep responses concise, then expand if asked.

## Mistakes to Avoid

- Giving generic answers without numbers.
- Over-focusing on activity volume instead of business outcomes.
- Criticizing past teams or customers.
- Ignoring cross-functional alignment with marketing, product, and customer success.
- Not asking clarifying questions in scenario prompts.
